## Overall Impression

The product has a clear design point of view — the arc gauge, the CAGR bar visual, the glass nav chrome — and the token system is coherent. But the core analysis surface (stock overview tab) is let down by two compounding problems: a six-card quality grid that defaults to Generic SaaS Dashboard scaffolding, and a contrast system with ~22 real gray-on-colored-background failures that undermine readability precisely where it matters most. The single biggest opportunity is to differentiate the quality grid — give it hierarchy, let it do synthesis rather than hand six equal verdicts to the user, and strip the eyebrow pattern that currently makes it read as AI-assembled.

## What's Working

**Arc gauge + animated fair value count-up.** The `InvestorGradeCard` grade arc is the product's strongest single design choice — a focal artifact unique to Intrinsico, emotionally resonant, and calibrated in its animation (spring scale + number count-up, not decorative spin). It is the peak moment in the product's emotional journey and the clearest expression of the brand: opinionated, confident, direct.

**`MarketPricingLeaderboard` CAGR mini-bars.** Side-by-side implied/historical mini-bars with semantic color encoding (green = conservative, red = aggressive) communicate the core product thesis — implied growth expectations vs history — in a single glance. This is the product doing what it claims: surfacing the market's implied view, not just raw numbers.

**Glass/solid surface discipline.** Glassmorphism is correctly scoped to nav chrome with full `@supports` fallbacks, `prefers-reduced-transparency` support, and solid-white fallbacks for data cards. This is rare — most implementations apply glass decoratively everywhere. The structural approach here is production-quality.

## Priority Issues

**[P1] Systemic gray-on-colored-background contrast failures (22 real instances, 17 files)**
- **Why it matters**: Gray text (`text-slate-400` to `text-slate-700`) on colored surfaces (`bg-blue-600`, `bg-emerald-500`, `bg-amber-500`, `bg-blue-50`) fails WCAG AA. The most severe: `text-slate-600 on bg-blue-600` in ForecastTable, PriceChart, and ai-stack — near-invisible on screen. For a product whose primary value is precision numbers, unreadable labels erode trust at the exact moments they matter. The `bg-blue-50` + `text-slate-400` pattern hits InvestorGradeCard, PriceHeader, YahooFinancials, FlipCard, HeroSearch, and landing pages.
- **Fix**: For elements on colored surfaces, use the hue-shifted muted approach: on `bg-blue-50` use `text-blue-600` (not `text-slate-400`); on `bg-blue-600` use `text-white` or `text-blue-100`. Audit all 22 confirmed instances; the full list is in the detector output.
- **Suggested command**: `/impeccable audit`

**[P1] Six-card OverviewMetricGrid renders as undifferentiated wall — identical grid structure, eyebrow pattern on every card**
- **Why it matters**: Six equal-weight cards with identical `CardHeader` eyebrows, identical shadow tokens, and identical `Details →` CTAs produce the exact Generic SaaS Dashboard impression the product brief names as an anti-reference. For the target user (financial analyst), seeing six co-equal boxes without synthesis means doing the synthesis work themselves — the product's stated job is "decisions, not just data."
- **Fix**: Establish grid hierarchy. The overall quality score (`ratings.overall`) already exists — lead with it as a single wide card, then break into grouped secondaries (Earnings Quality, Financial Health, Valuation). Strip the tracked uppercase eyebrow from within cards; the card container is already the label context. Replace `Details →` with tab-specific labels or remove the CTA entirely, relying on the tab system for navigation.
- **Suggested command**: `/impeccable layout`

**[P2] `/analyze` page shows the same 9 stocks twice (Popular Analyses cards + Leaderboard table), doubling cognitive load before the user has done anything**
- **Why it matters**: The page structure is: search → 9 cards → 9-row table (same stocks) → quick actions → history. Two representations of identical data double page scroll-weight without distinguishing their jobs. The Leaderboard table reaches the user only after they've already decided not to use the cards. Quick Actions — actually useful for discovery — is buried.
- **Fix**: Either merge the two sections (one primary data format, not two), or make them independent (cards = featured, leaderboard = different/full dataset). Elevate Quick Actions above the fold. The page's primary job is search; the browse content should scaffold discovery, not replicate it.
- **Suggested command**: `/impeccable layout`

**[P2] "Details →" CTA label on all six quality cards is semantically wrong for five of them**
- **Why it matters**: "Details →" on the Profitability card sends the user to the Risks tab, not a profitability detail view. This is a navigation mismatch — the user expects to deepen their view of profitability, not pivot to risks. Repeated five times, it degrades trust in the card navigation.
- **Fix**: Either link each card to its genuinely relevant sub-section of the Financials Hub, or remove the CTA entirely and let tab navigation handle flow. If the intent is "go deeper on this area," the label should say "See full analysis" and actually land somewhere relevant.
- **Suggested command**: `/impeccable clarify`

**[P2] Search friction: no keyboard shortcut to focus input, no visual affordance on empty submit, ETF source badges (`SPY`/`QQQ`/`DIA`) are unexplained internal taxonomy**
- **Why it matters**: Three compounding search usability gaps affect the product's primary entry point. No `/` or `Cmd+K` shortcut means power users must mouse to search — table stakes for a search-first product. Empty-query Enter is silently inert. The `etfSource` badge on every analysis card is an internal grouping label invisible to the investor — "why does my Apple analysis show 'SPY'?"
- **Fix**: Add a `useEffect` keyboard listener for `/` or `Cmd+K` to focus `inputRef.current`. Add a disabled visual state on the submit button when `query.trim() === ''`. Replace `etfSource` badge on cards with either "S&P 500" or remove it — it's not surfacing useful information at this level.
- **Suggested command**: `/impeccable polish`

## Persona Red Flags

**Alex (Power User)**
- No keyboard shortcut to focus search from anywhere on the `/analyze` page. Ticker symbols are short and typed from memory — the mouse path to search is friction.
- Tab state not persisted in URL. Bookmarking the Valuation tab for NVDA resets to Overview on return.
- At `xl:grid-cols-6`, each quality card is ~190px wide — the Altman Z-Score, ROIC spread, and interpretation sentence are cramped to ~13px at this width.

**Sam (Accessibility-Dependent)**
- "Remove from history" X button is `opacity-0 group-hover:opacity-100` at rest — keyboard-navigable but invisible without hover. WCAG 2.1 AA non-conformant focus discovery.
- `text-[9px]` in `StockAnalysisCard` for the ETF source badge and upside eyebrow labels — below any readable threshold at standard DPI.
- The `Info` tooltip icon is `size={9}` with `cursor-help` but no visible label or accessible ARIA; keyboard-only users cannot access it.
- 22 gray-on-colored-background failures directly affect Sam, who may be using high contrast or zoom mode where hue-shifted text reads less distinctly.

**Casey (Distracted Mobile User)**
- `OverviewMetricGrid` at `grid-cols-2` on mobile shows six cards at ~160px each, three rows of content-dense pairs. Very dense on a 375px screen.
- Horizontal scroll on Popular Analyses section has no scroll affordance signal; the snap behavior is invisible at first glance.
- "Remove from history" X button is entirely inaccessible on mobile (no hover state).

**The Analyst (financially literate investor, serious due diligence)**
- The "Preliminary · adjust assumptions in Valuation tab" caveat is inline body text at `text-[12px]`, same color as the verdict sentence — visually it reads as part of the conclusion rather than a caveat. High-stakes decisions deserve visually distinct caveats.
- Uneven tooltip coverage: ROIC vs WACC has an explanation tooltip; Altman Z-Score, Quick Ratio, and all six quality card metric rows do not. For an analyst unfamiliar with one metric, the gap creates trust asymmetry.
- Bear/base/bull scenario strip shows values but not the assumptions (CAGR, WACC, terminal growth rate) driving them. An analyst cannot validate the scenario range without navigating to the Valuation tab.
- No print/export path for the analysis output despite `@media print` CSS being present. An analyst sharing or filing a due diligence summary has no clean path.

## Minor Observations

- `StockAnalysisCard` ETF source badge (`SPY`, `QQQ`, `DIA`) is an internal taxonomy label on a user-facing card; most investors won't know what it maps to. Remove or replace with index name.
- The `MarketPricingLeaderboard` uses `STATIC_QUOTES` for implied CAGR data — always showing static values while `PopularAnalysesSection` loads live prices. Silent inconsistency: live prices overlay on static CAGR bars.
- `InvestorGradeCard` has a gradient CTA button (`bg-gradient-to-r from-blue-600 to-blue-500`) — the rest of the system uses flat `bg-blue-600`. Minor consistency gap, but the gradient button is a noted anti-pattern.
- Two `side-tab` violations found by detector: `border-l-4` in `components/markets/ModelAlerts.tsx:42` and `borderLeft: \`4px solid\`` in `app/ai-stack/report/page.tsx:253`. Both are the banned side-stripe accent pattern.
- `animate-bounce` in `components/markets/InstrumentPriceChart.tsx` and `NormalizedPerfChart.tsx` — bounce easing on data charts is distracting and non-purposeful.
- DM Mono and IBM Plex Sans are imported in font configuration but do not appear actively used in reviewed components — dead font weight in network request.
- The `"Blended from multiple models"` label at `text-[11px] text-slate-400` sits directly below the `"Fair Value Est."` eyebrow at `text-[11px] font-bold uppercase tracking-widest` — two lines of 11px text 4px apart with different weights and cases. The visual relationship between label and sub-label is ambiguous.

## Questions to Consider

1. **Is the "Popular Analyses" section serving the user or the product?** The nine featured stocks (top SPY/QQQ/DIA holdings) position Intrinsico as a market overview tool. But the page hero says "What do you want to analyze today?" — a search-first framing. If the core job is individual stock search, the browse section before the first search may be editorial noise that delays the task. If discovery is also a core job, the page needs two visually distinct modes rather than a vertical stack of both.

2. **Does the OverviewMetricGrid synthesize, or does it hand synthesis back to the user?** Six co-equal quality cards each show a grade badge — the user still has to integrate six verdicts into one view. The `ratings.overall` field already exists in the data model. If the product's thesis is "opinionated valuation," should the grid lead with the aggregate quality verdict and then break down supporting factors — rather than presenting six parallel assessments with no synthesis?

3. **What is the product's explicit contract with users about data freshness?** Leaderboard CAGR bars are static; fair values load with staggered delays; errors surface "Yahoo Finance may be temporarily unavailable." The product makes specific, precise numerical claims (fair value to two decimal places, CAGR to one decimal). When infrastructure shows seams, the precision of the numbers is undermined. Where does the product surface its data freshness and source policy in a way the analyst persona would find credible?
